Selenium and the role of defects for photovoltaic applications

Overview:

This project investigates the light absorption properties of one-dimensional selenium for photovoltaic applications. We focus on how defects—both intrinsic and extrinsic—affect its electronic properties and photovoltaic efficiency.

Data Sources:

The study is based on first-principles calculations of pristine trigonal selenium, exploring its band gap, optical absorption, and defect-related recombination behavior.

Tools and Technologies Used:

We used many-body perturbation theory (GW and BSE) for electronic property calculations and various functionals (PBE, HSE06, SCAN) to study defect formation energies and doping effects on selenium.

Key Findings:

Pristine selenium shows excellent agreement with experimental band gaps and optical absorption. While intrinsic defects have minimal impact on efficiency, extrinsic defects introduce deep levels that cause significant non-radiative recombination, reducing photovoltaic performance. These findings suggest improving synthesis conditions to limit extrinsic impurities can enhance selenium-based solar cell efficiency.
