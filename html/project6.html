Determining short-lived solid forms during phase transformations using molecular dynamics

Overview:

This project investigates short-lived crystal phases during the dehydration phase transition of the drug theophylline. Using molecular dynamics simulations, we identified high-energy, metastable phases that are critical for understanding phase transformations.

Data Sources:

Experimental data from PXRD and solid-state NMR were used to validate molecular dynamics simulation results of the theophylline dehydration process.

Tools and Technologies Used:

Molecular dynamics simulations were performed, with energy minimizations using DFT-D in the CASTEP software, combined with PXRD and NMR analysis.

Key Findings:

The study identified a short-lived, metastable crystal form during the dehydration process. The results suggest these transient phases are more common and important in understanding phase transitions than previously thought.
